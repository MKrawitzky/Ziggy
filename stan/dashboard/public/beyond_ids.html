The Three-Witness Rule CCS Adds Confidence

Every identification requires "witnesses" — independent dimensions of evidence. timsTOF adds CCS as a third witness that reduces false identification probability by orders of magnitude.

⚖️

1 Witness: m/z only

~1%

Traditional DDA/DIA. Peptide mass matched to database. ~1 in 100 IDs may be wrong at standard FDR cutoff.

⚖️⏱️

2 Witnesses: m/z + RT

~0.05%

Adds retention time as evidence. Peptides with wrong mass but matching RT are rejected. 20× more confident.

⚖️⏱️📐

3 Witnesses: m/z + RT + CCS

~0.001%

Adds collision cross-section (ion shape). A peptide must match all three physical properties simultaneously. 1,000× more confident than m/z alone.

Why this is important for your lab
CCS is a physical constant — like mass, it doesn't depend on your LC gradient, column brand, or lab temperature. A peptide identified by all three dimensions can be verified across labs and instruments. This makes CCS-confirmed identifications suitable for clinical and regulatory contexts where m/z-only identification would be challenged. An ID count without CCS filtering conflates high-confidence hits with speculative matches.
